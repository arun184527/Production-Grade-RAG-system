
What is RAG? – A system that combines retrieval of external data with LLM generation to produce accurate, grounded responses.

Why RAG is needed? – LLMs have limited knowledge and can hallucinate; RAG adds real data.

What does RAG solve? – Reduces hallucination and enables use of private or updated data.

What are core components of RAG? – Data ingestion, chunking, embeddings, vector DB, retrieval, and generation.

What is retrieval in RAG? – Fetching relevant documents using similarity search.

What is generation in RAG? – LLM generating answer using retrieved context.

What is knowledge base in RAG? – Collection of documents stored for retrieval.

What is grounding in RAG? – Ensuring answers are based on retrieved data.

What is hallucination? – LLM generating incorrect but confident answers.

How RAG reduces hallucination? – By providing real context from data.

What is data ingestion? – Loading raw data (PDF, text, JSON) into pipeline.

Why data cleaning needed? – Removes noise and improves embedding quality.

What is preprocessing? – Formatting and preparing data before chunking.

What formats used in RAG? – PDFs, TXT, JSON, HTML, databases.

What is document loader? – Tool to read and extract data (e.g., LangChain loaders).

What is text extraction? – Converting PDF or files into plain text.

What is noisy data? – Irrelevant or messy content affecting retrieval.

Why remove special characters? – Improves embedding consistency.

What is deduplication? – Removing repeated data.

Why structured data helpful? – Easier to chunk and retrieve.

What is chunking? – Splitting documents into smaller parts.

Why chunking important? – Improves retrieval precision.

What is chunk size? – Number of tokens per chunk.

What is overlap? – Shared content between chunks.

Why overlap used? – Maintains context continuity.

What is semantic chunking? – Splitting based on meaning.

What is fixed chunking? – Equal size splitting.

What is recursive chunking? – Hierarchical splitting method.

What is chunk metadata? – Info like source, page, section.

What is chunk quality? – How meaningful and self-contained chunk is.

What are embeddings? – Vector representations of text.

Why embeddings needed? – Enable semantic similarity search.

What is embedding model? – Converts text into vectors.

What is vector dimension? – Size of embedding vector.

What is semantic similarity? – Similar meaning = similar vectors.

What is cosine similarity? – Measures angle similarity between vectors.

Why normalize embeddings? – Improves comparison accuracy.

What is sentence embedding? – Vector of full sentence.

What is document embedding? – Vector of full document.

What is multilingual embedding? – Supports multiple languages.

What is vector DB? – Stores embeddings for fast retrieval.

Why vector DB used? – Enables semantic search.

What is FAISS? – Local vector search library.

What is Pinecone? – Managed cloud vector DB.

What is indexing? – Organizing vectors for fast search.

What is ANN? – Approximate nearest neighbor search.

Why ANN used? – Faster search with large data.

What is top-k retrieval? – Getting most similar k results.

What is metadata filtering? – Filtering results using extra info.

What is hybrid search? – Combining keyword + vector search.
