What is an LLM? – A Large Language Model trained on massive text data to understand and generate human-like language.

Why are LLMs powerful? – They capture language patterns, context, and reasoning from large-scale training.

What is NLP? – Field of AI focused on understanding and generating human language.

What is a language model? – Model that predicts the next word/token in a sequence.

What is pretraining? – Training on large general datasets to learn language patterns.

What is fine-tuning? – Training on specific data to adapt model behavior.

What is generative AI? – AI that creates new content like text, images, or code.

What is autoregressive model? – Generates text one token at a time.

What is token? – Smallest unit of text processed by model.

What is context in LLM? – Previous tokens used to generate next output.

What is tokenization? – Splitting text into smaller units like words or subwords.

Why tokenization needed? – Models cannot process raw text directly.

What is subword tokenization? – Breaking words into smaller meaningful parts.

What is BPE? – Byte Pair Encoding method for tokenization.

What is WordPiece? – Tokenization method used in BERT.

What is tokenizer vocabulary? – Set of tokens known to the model.

What is OOV token? – Out-of-vocabulary token not recognized.

Why token count important? – Affects cost and context window.

What is special token? – Tokens like [CLS], [SEP], or EOS.

What is padding? – Adding tokens to match input length.

What is embedding? – Vector representation of tokens.

Why embeddings used? – Convert text into numerical format.

What is positional encoding? – Adds order information to tokens.

Why positional encoding needed? – Transformers lack sequence awareness.

What is embedding layer? – First layer mapping tokens to vectors.

What is contextual embedding? – Meaning depends on surrounding words.

What is static embedding? – Same vector regardless of context.

Why contextual embeddings better? – Capture dynamic meanings.

What is hidden state? – Internal representation of token in model.

What is representation learning? – Learning useful data features automatically.

What is transformer architecture? – Deep learning model using attention mechanism.

Why transformers replaced RNNs? – Better parallelism and long-range dependency handling.

What is attention mechanism? – Focus on relevant parts of input.

What is self-attention? – Tokens attend to each other.

What are Q, K, V? – Query, Key, Value matrices used in attention.

What is attention score? – Importance of one token to another.

What is softmax in attention? – Converts scores into probabilities.

What is multi-head attention? – Multiple attention layers capturing different patterns.

What is feedforward layer? – Fully connected layer after attention.

What is layer normalization? – Stabilizes training.

What is encoder? – Processes input and builds representation.

What is decoder? – Generates output sequence.

What is encoder-only model? – Used for understanding tasks (e.g., BERT).

What is decoder-only model? – Used for generation (e.g., GPT).

What is encoder-decoder model? – Used for translation tasks.

Why GPT is decoder-only? – Designed for text generation.

Why BERT is encoder-only? – Designed for understanding context.

What is masked language model? – Predict missing tokens (BERT).

What is causal language model? – Predict next token (GPT).

What is seq2seq model? – Maps input sequence to output sequence.

What is training objective? – Minimize prediction error.

What is loss function? – Measures difference between prediction and actual.

What is cross-entropy loss? – Common loss for language models.

What is gradient descent? – Optimization method to update weights.

What is backpropagation? – Computes gradients for updates.

What is optimizer? – Algorithm like Adam for training.

What is learning rate? – Step size for updates.

What is epoch? – One full pass of dataset.

What is batch size? – Number of samples per update.

What is overfitting? – Model memorizes training data.

What is inference? – Using trained model to generate output.

What is decoding? – Process of generating tokens.

What is greedy decoding? – Picks highest probability token.

What is beam search? – Explores multiple sequences.

What is temperature? – Controls randomness in output.

What is top-k sampling? – Limits selection to top k tokens.

What is top-p sampling? – Chooses tokens based on cumulative probability.

Why sampling needed? – To make output more diverse.

What is deterministic output? – Same input gives same output.

What is stochastic output? – Output varies due to randomness.
